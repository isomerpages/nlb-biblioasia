#### <a style="text-decoration: none; font-weight: bold;" href="/vol-17/issue-4/jan-to-mar-2022/asia-maps-cartography" target="_blank">Mapping the World: Perspectives from Asian Cartography</a> 
<p>Mapping the World: Perspectives from Asian Cartography brings together different mapping traditions and worldviews, and political and cultural spheres of influence. It also explores the exchange of cartographic knowledge between civilisations across the world. Written by curators Pierre Singaravélou and Fabrice Argounès, this book showcases masterpieces in cartographic history from various institutions and invites us to appreciate the beauty and diversity of Asian cartography.</p>

<p>A little-known collection from 1818 to 1820 commissioned under the watch of two French naturalists sheds light on the early study of the region’s flora and fauna.</p>

#### <a style="text-decoration: none; font-weight: bold;" href="https://nlb.overdrive.com/media/6520148" target="_blank">Chapters on Asia: Selected Papers from the Lee Kong Chian Research Fellowship (2020)</a> 
<i>Chapters on Asia</i> features selected papers written by scholars who have been awarded the National Library's Lee Kong Chian Research Fellowship. This 2020 edition features papers written by Anh Sy Huy Le (on Chinese rice commerce in colonial Vietnam), Lee Chor Lin (on the prewar and postwar art scene in Singapore) and Mohamed Qusairy Bin Thaha (in Malay on the writings of Ahmad Sonhadji Mohamad Melalui Khutbah).
